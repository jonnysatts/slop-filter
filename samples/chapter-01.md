The corridor had the kind of silence that seemed to press against the skin. Mara paused with one hand on the latch and listened again, not for footsteps but for the scrape of metal she had heard an hour earlier and tried to forget. It didn't come.

She opened the door a fraction. The room beyond smelled of lamp oil and wet wool. A ledger lay open on the table, its pages fanned by the draft from the cracked window. Someone had left in a hurry. Someone who thought they would be back.

Mara crossed to the table and read the last line twice.

The shipment had arrived early. The key had changed hands. North stairwell after bells.

She shut the ledger, slid it beneath her coat, and only then heard the boards creak behind her.

Jonah hated the harbour at dawn, though he came here whenever he needed to think. The gulls were too loud, the ropes too damp, the men too eager to look busy before anyone had asked them for proof of it. Even the light felt argumentative, all tin and glare and sharp edges on the water.

He counted three customs officers near the east pier and a fourth pretending not to watch the warehouse doors. That made the morning worse, but only slightly. Worse had already arrived in the form of Mara's note, folded into a square so tight it looked less like correspondence than a warning.

Burn this. Meet me before second bell. Bring no one.

He read it once, then fed it to the lantern flame and watched the paper twist black at the corners before it vanished.

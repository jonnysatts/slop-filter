# The Glass Harbor

Needless to say, Mara knew that the harbour had changed since winter, though nobody in the market would name it aloud.

The cranes stood over the water like bent saints, and the ropes knocked against the masts with a patient little clatter. Jonah was standing by the ticket office and he was looking at the ferries as if the grey paint itself had something to confess.

It was a pivotal moment, or so it felt to Mara, but the cold on the rail mattered more than the idea. She put her palm on the metal, listened to the gulls, and waited for him to turn.
